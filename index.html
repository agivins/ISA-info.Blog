Information Security Analyst Overview

what is an Information Security Analyst?

With the rise in hackers and data breaches, there is a great need to keep personal information safe from cyberattacks. An Information Security Analyst can be described as someone who protects computer networks at companies, nonprofits, and government agencies. An Information Security Analyst works in computer systems design services, finance, insurance, and administrative support services. Individuals who are Information Security Analysts usually develop, implement, and ensure compliance policies to protect the system.

responsibility

Information Security Analyst Responsibilities Includes:

  • Monitor computer networks for security issues.
  • Investigate security breaches and other security incidents.
  • Install security measures and operate software to protect systems.
  • Monitor document security breaches and assess.
  • Work with security team to perform tests and uncover network vulnerabilities.
  • Fix detected vulnerabilities
  • Maintain a high-security standard.
  • Stay updated on IT security trends and news.
  • Develop company-wide best practices for IT security.
  • Perform penetration testing.
  • Install security software and understand information security management.
  • Research security enhancements and make recommendations to management.
  • Stay up-to-date on information technology security standards.

    • education

    Information Security Analysts usually need at least a bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Information Technology, Management Information Systems, or a related field. According to Careerexplorer.com Information Security Analysts often have similar levels of education. 85% of Information Security Analysts have a bachelor's degree, with the second most common being a certificate or associate degree at 12%. Some employers prefer applicants who have a Master of Business Administration (MBA) in Information Systems. Programs offering the MBA in Information Systems generally require 2 years of study beyond the undergraduate level and includes both business and computer-related courses. In addition to a MBA, some employers may also require a certain number of years of experience.
